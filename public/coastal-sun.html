UV Index Guidance

Plan your outdoor time wisely with our real-time UV index readings for Delaware beaches and beyond. Understanding the ultraviolet radiation level helps you take the right precautions, whether you're heading to Rehoboth Beach, Cape Henlopen, or your own backyard.

0-2

Low

Minimal risk. Safe to be outside unprotected for extended periods. Ideal for beach walks and casual gardening.

3-5

Moderate

Take care during midday hours. Wear sunscreen and sunglasses. Seek shade during peak sun intensity.

6-7

High

Protection essential. Reduce time in the sun between 10 a.m. and 4 p.m. Use SPF 30+ and protective clothing.

8-11+

Very High to Extreme

Unprotected skin can burn in minutes. Avoid being outside during midday. Seek shade and use SPF 50+.

Midday Awareness

UV rays are strongest between 10 a.m. and 4 p.m. Even on cloudy days, up to 80% of UV radiation can reach your skin. When planning outdoor activities along Delaware's coast, check the UV index alongside our live local weather radar for the most complete picture before heading out.

Delaware Tide Times

Understanding the rhythm of Delaware's coastal tides is essential for planning a successful day on the water. Whether you're heading out for a fishing charter, launching a boat, enjoying a beach day, or managing waterfront operations, our detailed tide windows help you make informed decisions. We track high and low tide predictions for the state's most popular coastal points so you can coordinate your activities with the natural ebb and flow of the Atlantic and Delaware Bay.

Tide predictions are based on astronomical data and local observational models. Always check for updated local weather radar live feeds before heading out, as storm surges and wind patterns can significantly alter expected water levels. For those searching for a local weather radar near me, our interactive tools provide real-time context alongside these tide schedules.

Bay Conditions & Shoreline Planning: Delaware Bay points often experience wind-driven tide shifts. Always monitor local marine forecasts and use our local weather radar to track approaching systems before navigating shallow inlets.
